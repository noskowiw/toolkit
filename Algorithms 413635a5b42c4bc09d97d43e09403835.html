Algorithms

In short:

  • choosing the right algorithm or data structure is the most significant part of making your program energy efficient
  • Most commonly-used algorithms and data structures have been implemented in almost all programming languages, so there is a good chance that a ready-made solution exists that you can use.
  • Parts of your codebase that experience the most traffic are most likely to benefit from algorithmic optimization.

Almost every problem in programming has more than one functionally-sound solution. Although all of these approaches deliver the same correct output, their energy efficiency and time of execution can vary widely. In most cases this difference in performance comes down to the types of algorithms and data structures these approaches are using.

In cloud computing, where resources can be scaled at will, algorithmic inefficiencies can be addressed by provisioning more resources. This, however, causes a significantly decrease in energy efficiency, therefore making the solution in question less sustainable.

Finding the right algorithm or a data structure for the job is rarely a trivial task. Changg the algorithmic structure of the program can also require a significant amount of refactoring. For those reasons it is important to direct your focus to the parts of your codebase that are most likely to benefit from optimization, those being:

  • fragments of code that experience the most amount of traffic
  • functions that perform complex/time consuming computations

There’s a large body of resources out there that can help you find the algorithmic solution that suit your needs best.

Tips:

If you’re having trouble finding the right approach for your specific scenario, it might be helpful to come up with a simplified version of the problem and try to find a well documented best approach for solving it.
