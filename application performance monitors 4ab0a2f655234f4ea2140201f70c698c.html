Application Performance Monitors

In short:

  • APMs allow you to monitor your application’s behavior at a high level, helping you to identify bottlenecks or other areas that can be refactored in order to increase the energy efficiency of your software.
  • There are plenty of tools to choose from, so you can find the one that best suits the needs of your project.
  • Bigger applications are the most likely to benefit from implementing APMs.

Application Performance Monitors are software tools that help identify performance issues in highly complex applications. They track and analyze various metrics, such as response time, throughput, and error rate, and display them in a comprehensible manner. By monitoring these metrics, you can identify resource-intensive elements and inefficiencies in your application. By focusing your refactoring efforts, you can greatly improve your application’s energy efficiency, therefore making it more sustainable.

Example of an amp dashboard source: https://logz.io/blog/monitoring-microservices-opentelemetry-jaeger/

Larger distributed projects or those that utilize the microservices are the most likely to benefit from the use of these applications.

These programs can be difficult to add to existing solutions, so the decision to implement them should be made during the design stage.
